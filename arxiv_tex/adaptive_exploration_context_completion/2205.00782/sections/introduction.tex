\vic{In recent years, the multi-hop reasoning problem of answering first-order logic queries (FOL) on large-scale incomplete knowledge graphs (KGs)~\cite{Pan2016}  has  gained a lot of attention in the AI community.}
%Knowledge Graphs (KGs)~\cite{Pan2016,Pan2017} are heterogeneous graph-structured knowledge bases, where knowledge is organized in a form of (\emph{h,r,t}) triples, meaning head entity \emph{h} is related to tail entity \emph{t-} through relationship \emph{r}~\cite{wang2021relational}. Multi-hop reasoning over KGs aims to find answer entities of given queries using knowledge from KGs~\cite{zhang2021cone}.

 \vicf{To address these challenges, a \emph{query embedding (QE)} approach to query answering has been recently introduced as an alternative to subgraph matching methods. The main idea %behind the QE approach

\par Our main contributions can be summarized as follows:
\begin{compactitem}
\item \vic{We propose \textsc{Temp}, a novel \emph{type-aware plug-and-play} model for multi-hop reasoning \jeff{over}   KGs, %which simultaneously utilizes the type information of entities and relations, and 
that can be easily incorporated into the existing QE-based models.}% \textit{To our best knowledge, this is first work to leverage type information for multi-hop reasoning over KGs}. }
\item \vic{We design a new bidirectional integration mechanism that incorporates the pairwise dependencies among  \{entity, relation, type\} information, even in the absence of schema   axioms like domain and range.} %to further strengthen the intersection  ability between different representations.}
\item \vic{Extensive experiments demonstrate that after incorporating \textsc{Temp} into four  state-of-the-art baselines, their \emph{ generalization}, \emph{deductive} and \emph{inductive reasoning}  abilities are significantly improved across three benchmark datasets consistently.}
\end{compactitem}
