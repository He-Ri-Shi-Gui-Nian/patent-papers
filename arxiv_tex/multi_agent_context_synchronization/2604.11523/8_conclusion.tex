\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this work, we introduce \ours, a benchmark for evaluating multi-agent collaboration under owner-defined privacy constraints.
By explicitly modeling ownership, structured information asymmetry, and constraint-governed disclosure, \ours reveals systematic gaps between task success and collaboration success.
Our analysis shows that current agents often prioritize short-term task progress over faithful privacy constraint compliance, even in minimal two-owner settings.
These findings highlight the need to move beyond task-centric evaluation and toward benchmarks and agent designs that treat ownership and privacy constraint adherence as first-class objectives in collaborative private agents.
