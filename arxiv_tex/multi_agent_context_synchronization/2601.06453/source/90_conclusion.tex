\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We propose a multi-agent collaboration framework for multimodal sensing that decomposes sensing tasks into modality-aware agents, enabling independent interpretation of heterogeneous sensor streams. Our hybrid fusion mechanism navigates the trade-off between semantic aggregation and statistical consensus of the modality-specific outputs. While semantic reasoning remains robust under sensor failures, the statistical anchor mitigates the knowledge bias often observed in LLM-based judges that over-prioritize specific modalities. Evaluations across five diverse sensing benchmarks confirm that \system{} outperforms the single-agent baseline by an average of $7.1\%$ in accuracy. Furthermore, by employing a single-round hybrid fusion protocol, \system{} achieves a $12.7\times$ reduction in fusion token costs while matching the performance of state-of-the-art multi-agent debate methods. In conclusion, \system{} provides a scalable, efficient, and robust solution for grounding LLMs in the physical world.
