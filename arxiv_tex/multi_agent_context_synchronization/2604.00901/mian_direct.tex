Table~\ref{tab:part1-data-swapped} highlights the performance of \hero compared to direct and CoT inference without RAG. Across all datasets, \hero exhibits substantial gains over baseline LLMs, with improvements particularly pronounced on multi-hop and ambiguous QA tasks. Notably, HERA (\qwen) consistently outperforms HERA (\llama), reflecting its superior capability in complex reasoning scenarios. Across metrics, \hero achieves more than double the F1 scores of standard CoT baselines on several datasets, indicating that experience-guided orchestration and prompt evolution provide strong advantages even without retrieval augmentation. Gains are also robust across both in-domain and sparse or ambiguous queries, demonstrating that structured inter-agent coordination and semantic group-level reasoning significantly enhance reasoning fidelity over single-agent inference.
