\section{Background and Related Work}

\paragraph{LLM Cultural Alignment.}
The accuracy of LLM predictions across diverse cultural contexts has received increased attention. One line of work examines the cross-cultural difference in moral values and beliefs encoded in LLMs and their alignment with diverse human perspectives \cite{moralstories, moralencoded, jinnai-2024-cross, cao-etal-2023-assessing, arora-etal-2023-probing, trollymultilingual}. Another explores how LLMs interpret social etiquette and norms across varying cultural contexts \cite{farperspective, normdial, renovi, culturebank, normad, normbank}. Building on these works, our study focuses specifically on the cultural alignment of LLMs with social norms, using \textsc{NormAd-eti} \cite{normad} benchmark as a testbed.

Extensive efforts have also been made to enhance cultural alignment in LLMs, encompassing advancements in pre-training \cite{acegpt, seallm, wang-etal-2024-countries}, alignment training \cite{choenni-etal-2024-echoes, culturellm, mukherjee-etal-2024-global}, and inference strategies, such as effective prompt design \cite{investigating, cao-etal-2023-assessing, shen-etal-2024-understanding} and in-context learning \cite{selfalignment, lahoti-etal-2023-improving}. However, prior work has primarily focused on single LLMs in single-turn interactions, with the use of multiple LLMs under-explored. The closest work to ours is CulturePark \cite{culturepark}, which leverages an LLM-powered multi-agent communication framework for cultural data collection. We depart from this work by adapting a multi-agent debate framework specifically to enhance cultural alignment, presenting a new perspective within model inference strategies.


\paragraph{Multi-Agent Debate.}
The diverse perspectives of LLMs arise from differences in training data and alignment processes, making them well-suited for pluralistic alignment tasks \cite{modular}. Inspired by human reasoning, prior works have proposed frameworks where multiple LLMs generate responses, process others' opinions, and engage in debates to reach consensus \cite{estornell2024multillm}. This collaborative approach has shown potential in problem-solving by combining knowledge and extracting diverse perspectives \cite{farperspective}. Most works focus on improving factuality and reasoning capabilities, where they show that debate reduce the tendency of LLMs to hallucinate incorrect facts through ``\textit{society of minds}'' interaction \cite{du2023improvingfactualityreasoninglanguage} or encourage divergent thinking \cite{liang-etal-2024-encouraging} in arithmetic reasoning, question-answering, and translation tasks. Other studies used multi-agent debate frameworks to evaluate LLM-generated responses \cite{chateval} or to represent different nationalities for constructing culturally specific debate datasets \cite{culturepark}. However, no prior work has specifically employed a multi-agent debate framework to enhance cultural alignment in LLMs, and our work aims to fill this gap.
